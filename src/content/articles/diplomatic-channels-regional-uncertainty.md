Diplomatic contacts between several governments have continued through informal channels despite a pause in scheduled formal negotiations, according to sources familiar with the process. Representatives described the conversations as exploratory rather than substantive, aimed at maintaining communication during a period of heightened uncertainty.

External mediators have offered facilitation services, though no parties have publicly confirmed engagement with these offers. Observers monitoring the situation noted that the continuation of back-channel contacts is generally regarded as a positive indicator.

Scheduled multilateral meetings later in the quarter are expected to provide a more structured opportunity to assess whether conditions for renewed formal engagement are in place. Analysts cautioned against reading too much into the current pace of dialogue.
