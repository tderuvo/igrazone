Several governments have initiated reviews of existing bilateral and multilateral technology cooperation agreements, according to statements issued by relevant ministries this week. The reviews cover areas including data localisation requirements, interoperability standards, and joint research protocols.

Officials indicated that the reviews are part of broader strategic assessments rather than responses to specific disputes. Working-level consultations are ongoing, with formal conclusions expected to inform policy updates later in the year.

Technology sector observers noted that the outcome of these reviews could have downstream effects on cross-border data flows and the development of shared technical standards. Industry groups have submitted position papers to several of the reviewing governments.
